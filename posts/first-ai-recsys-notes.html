Notes from building an AI search recommender

A realistic snapshot of what worked when shipping recommendation systems.

May 2026 Tags: Recsys · RAG · Ranking Reading time: 4 min

While working as a Junior AI Engineer at First AI, I helped build recommendation and ranking pipelines for AI search. The work was less about fancy models and more about reliable feedback loops, fast iteration, and measurable impact.

What I learned quickly

  • Start with clear evaluation metrics (precision@k, recall@k, diversity).
  • Personalization only helps if you have clean user signals.
  • Retrieval quality matters more than the final generator.
  • Offline evaluation saves weeks of online experimentation.

How we improved results

We focused on a lightweight reranker, improved feature logging, and added a daily evaluation report. The combination made it easy to see regressions and validate new ideas quickly.

Takeaway

The most impactful work was not a single model, but a reliable system: good data, clear metrics, and repeatable experiments.
