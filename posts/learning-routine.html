A learning routine that sticks

How I balance studying, coding, and research projects.

May 2026 Tags: Learning · Systems Reading time: 4 min

The routine that worked for me is simple: learn, apply, and reflect. I pick one learning goal per week, apply it in a small project, then document what I learned.

Weekly structure

  • Mon–Tue: learn new concepts and outline notes.
  • Wed–Thu: apply in a small prototype or project branch.
  • Fri: review, document, and share results.

Why this helps

Applying knowledge quickly helps me retain it. Writing short summaries forces clarity and keeps the learning loop active.
