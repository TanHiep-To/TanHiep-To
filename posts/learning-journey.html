Learning Journey

My learning journey in AI

From foundational coursework to research-driven GenAI systems.

May 2026 Tags: Learning · AI · MSc Reading time: 4 min

My academic path began firmly rooted in core computer science foundations—mastering algorithms, data structures, and distributed systems. The pivotal turning point occurred during my undergraduate research in Generative AI, where I first experienced the thrill of teaching machines to create. This passion naturally evolved into a focused MSc track in AI and Machine Learning at VNU-HCM, blending rigorous theory with practical applications.

Structured Learning Phases

I view my learning journey not as a straight line, but as building layers of capability:

  • Foundation Layer: Establishing a rock-solid base in CS fundamentals, clean software engineering practices, and the underlying mathematics (linear algebra, calculus, statistics) essential for Machine Learning.
  • Specialization Layer: Diving deep into specific domains like deep learning architectures, computer vision pipelines, and Natural Language Processing (NLP) research.
  • Applied Research Layer: Moving beyond tutorials to tackle complex systems: optimizing Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) architectures, parameter-efficient LLM fine-tuning (LoRA, QLoRA), and building robust evaluation pipelines.
  • Production Layer: The ongoing challenge of bridging the gap between isolated research scripts and reliable, scalable AI services that run in production environments.

Key Milestones and Achievements

  • Academic Excellence: Graduated with honors and achieved a perfect 10/10 score on my undergraduate thesis focused on advancing Generative AI techniques.
  • Lab Research: Actively contributed to AI research labs, exploring the nuances of diffusion models (Stable Diffusion) and optimizing RAG workflows for enterprise applications.
  • Industry Impact: Successfully transitioned applied research into industry settings, developing high-performance recommender systems and deploying machine learning models for proactive cybersecurity threat detection.

What Accelerated My Growth

Theory is essential, but project-based learning and hands-on experimentation accelerated my understanding exponentially. Mentorship from senior researchers in the lab, combined with the pressure of shipping real, user-facing systems at work, provided the perfect crucible for growth.

Each internship and full-time role taught me a crucial industry reality: how to balance the theoretical pursuit of model perfection with the practical constraints of product impact, latency, and operational cost.

The most enduring lesson from this journey? Consistency beats intensity. Keep the learning loop simple: aggressively learn new concepts, immediately apply them in code, meticulously document the findings, and openly share the knowledge with the community.
